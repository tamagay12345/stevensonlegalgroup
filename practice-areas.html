Practice 01

Family & Marriage Law

Practical and compassionate legal advice for separation, parenting, support and significant family transitions.

Family disputes can affect children, housing, finances, property, personal safety and long-term stability. Clients need legal advice that recognizes both the emotional weight of the matter and the importance of a carefully structured legal strategy.

Stevenson Legal Group LLP helps clients understand their rights and responsibilities, organize the information required for informed decision-making and evaluate negotiation, mediation and litigation options.

  • Separation and divorce planning
  • Parenting time and decision-making
  • Child support and spousal support
  • Property division and disclosure
  • Marriage and separation agreements
  • Urgent motions and protective measures

How the firm may assist

The firm identifies the central legal issues, establishes practical priorities and works toward a durable resolution while remaining prepared for court proceedings where necessary.

Practice 02

Criminal Defence

Prompt and discreet defence advice when liberty, reputation and future opportunities may be at risk.

Being investigated, arrested or charged can create immediate uncertainty. Statements, release conditions, court appearances and evidence-related decisions may significantly affect the direction of the matter.

The firm reviews the allegations, available disclosure, procedural history and potential legal issues while helping the client prepare for each stage of the process.

  • Pre-charge and investigation advice
  • Bail and release-related matters
  • Disclosure and evidence review
  • Charter and procedural concerns
  • Resolution discussions and negotiation
  • Trial preparation and courtroom advocacy

How the firm may assist

The firm responds promptly, preserves confidentiality, examines the prosecution’s position and prepares a defence grounded in the evidence and applicable law.

Practice 03

Real Estate & Property Law

Clear legal guidance for transactions, ownership concerns and disputes involving valuable property interests.

Real estate matters frequently involve strict deadlines, detailed agreements and substantial financial commitments. A careful legal review may identify title concerns, contractual exposure or ownership issues before they become more difficult to resolve.

Stevenson Legal Group LLP assists with transaction planning, document review and disputes involving ownership, access, agreements and other property interests.

  • Residential purchase and sale matters
  • Agreement and document review
  • Title and ownership concerns
  • Co-ownership and partition disputes
  • Boundary, access and easement issues
  • Failed transactions and contract claims

How the firm may assist

The firm reviews the legal structure of the transaction or dispute, identifies exposure and develops a practical approach designed to protect the client’s property interest.

Practice 04

Civil Litigation

Strategic representation for contract, financial, business and property-related disputes.

Effective litigation requires more than filing documents. It requires a clear theory of the case, reliable evidence, careful risk assessment and a plan for each procedural stage.

The firm evaluates the legal and practical considerations affecting the dispute, including cost, timing, evidence, damages, enforceability and possible settlement opportunities.

  • Contract and payment disputes
  • Business and commercial claims
  • Property-related litigation
  • Negligence and financial loss claims
  • Demand letters and settlement negotiation
  • Motions, hearings and trial preparation

How the firm may assist

The firm identifies the strongest available arguments, builds the evidentiary record and pursues a proportionate resolution through negotiation or litigation.

Practice 05

Estate Planning

Thoughtful planning to protect personal instructions, family interests and the administration of an estate.

A properly considered estate plan can help reduce uncertainty, protect intended beneficiaries and provide clear guidance to the people responsible for carrying out important decisions.

The firm helps clients examine family circumstances, assets, decision-making needs and administration concerns before preparing or updating estate documents.

  • Wills and estate-planning reviews
  • Powers of attorney
  • Executor and trustee considerations
  • Beneficiary and family concerns
  • Estate administration guidance
  • Planning after significant life changes

How the firm may assist

The firm translates the client’s instructions into clear legal documents and identifies issues that could otherwise create uncertainty, delay or conflict.
